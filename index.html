Product Carbon Footprint

A product carbon footprint (or PCF) is the calculation of all the greenhouse gas (GHG) emmisions generated in the supply chain of a specific product

How we do it:

Our company employs a cradle to grave analysis calculates the carbon footprints of products from the extration of raw materials (cradle) to the disposal (grave).

A refined energy product (source: carbonchain)

there are 2 kinds of data required to calculate the PCF:

  1. information about the production processes(with boundries set out)
  2. Emissions data (usually emmisions factors)
example of process map for a steel product.(source: carbonchain)

To finally calculate PCF, the formula activity * emmisions factorfor every activity,to develop the product's emissions inventory.

Where We Get Our Data:
  • The CDP (formerly the Carbon Disclosure Project) is an international non-profit organisation based in the United Kingdom, Japan, India, China, Germany, Brazil and the United States that helps companies, cities, states, regions and public authorities disclose their environmental impact. It aims to make environmental reporting and risk management a business norm, driving disclosure, insight, and action towards a sustainable economy.

  • Open Government Data (OGD) platforms

  • Information from investors, carbon footprinting websites and third party information
