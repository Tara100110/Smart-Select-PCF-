Product Carbon Footprint

A product carbon footprint (or PCF) is the calculation of all the greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions generated in the supply chain of a specific product

How We Do It:

Our company employs a cradle-to-grave analysis that calculates the carbon footprints of products from the extraction of raw materials (cradle) to the disposal (grave).

There are 2 kinds of data required to calculate the PCF:

Information about the production processes (with boundaries set out)

Emissions data (usually emissions factors)

To finally calculate PCF, the formula activity * emissions factor for every activity is used to develop the product's emissions inventory.

Where We Get Our Data:

The CDP (formerly the Carbon Disclosure Project) is an international non-profit organisation that helps companies, cities, states, regions, and public authorities disclose their environmental impact. It aims to make environmental reporting and risk management a business norm, driving disclosure, insight, and action towards a sustainable economy.
